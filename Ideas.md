**High-impact, low-risk modernizations:**

1. **Skill combos/cooldown-based combat** instead of spam-click one skill. Add 4-6 skill slots on cooldown (D4/PoE style). Makes combat feel active instead of a macro.

2. **Better boss fights** — telegraphed AoE attacks, phases, dodge windows. Original MU bosses are just bullet sponges with high damage. This alone would feel next-gen while keeping the grind identity.

3. **Visual loot rarity** — color-coded glow/beam on drops (white/blue/gold/red) so rare drops feel like an event, not just text you have to read.

4. **Smart auto-pickup** for zen/common trash items, manual pickup only for gear. Cuts busywork, doesn't cut the core loop.

5. **Shared stash / account warehouse** — modern QoL, doesn't touch nostalgia at all, everyone will thank you.

6. **Better minimap/waypoints** — MU's map UI is garbage by modern standards. Fix it, nobody will miss the old one.

7. **Seasonal/ladder resets** (like D2/D4/PoE) — fresh economy, new players can compete, keeps long-term retention way better than a static eternal server.

8. **Modernized socket/craft UI** — the mechanic stays, but drag-drop with clear success chance visualization instead of cryptic menus.

9. **Party finder / LFG tool** — MU grinding was always more fun in groups but finding a party was painful. Built-in tool = huge QoL win, zero nostalgia cost.

10. **Dynamic events in the open world** — random elite spawns, mini-events while grinding (like D4 world events). Keeps the same maps/mobs but adds surprise on top of the grind.

Skip: transmog, battle pass, gacha mechanics — those fight the "authentic MU economy" feel you're trying to preserve.
